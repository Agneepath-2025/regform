# **Cricket** 

*Mens*

**Format:**

1. Direct knockout format or Group stage *(subject to change; contingent on number of teams participating)*

**Match Rules:**

1. White ball tournament  
2. The minimum squad size is 12 participants.  
3. The maximum squad size is 15 participants.  
4. The umpire’s decision is final  
5.  One innings per team  
   1. Number of overs per inning *(subject to change; contingent on number of teams participating)*  
   2. Each over comprises 6 deliveries.  
6. Both innings are to be completed to obtain a result.  
   1. In case of a draw after the completion of both innings, the result will be determined via a super over.  
   2. Super overs continue till a winner is determined.  
   3. In case of a super over, captains of both teams must submit a list of 3 batsmen and 1 bowler to the umpires before the super over begins. In case of a subsequent super over, this list can be renewed or changed.  
7. Powerplay  
   1. The number of overs for the batting powerplay is contingent on the number of total overs in the match.
   2. For the duration of the powerplay, the fielding team can only have up to 2 fielders outside the 30-yard circle.  
   3. After the powerplay, the fielding team can have up to 5 fielders outside the 30-yard circle.  
   4. Failure to comply with these fielding regulations will result in a no-ball being called.  
8. Over Rate  
   1. The fielding team must complete their overs in  
      1. 65 minutes in case of a 15-over innings  
      2. 90 minutes in case of a 20-over innings  
   2. In case of a slow over rate, one fielder goes back in the circle for the remaining overs.  
   3. Allowances will be made for dismissals (2 minutes added for each wicket)  
9. Number of overs per bowler  
   1. A team is expected to have a minimum of 5 bowlers bowled at the end of an innings.  
   2. A particular bowler can therefore bowl a maximum of  
      1. 3 overs in case of a 15-over innings  
      2. 4 overs in case of a 20-over innings  
10. Dead ball  
    1. A ball will be called dead on the occasion of  
       1. Ball settling in the hands of the wicket-keeper or the bowler  
       2. A boundary being scored  
       3. A dismissal  
       4. Ball becoming trapped between the bat and person of a batsman, between items of his clothing or equipment, or the clothing of an umpire, whether hit or not  
11. Modes of dismissal  
    1. Bowled  
    2. Caught Out  
    3. Run Out  
    4. Hit Wicket  
    5. Hit the ball twice deliberately   
    6. Stumped  
    7. Obstructing the field deliberately   
    8. Timed Out  
    9. The new batsman not being present at the crease within 2 minutes of the previous batsman being dismissed.  
12. Chucking  
    1. A no-ball will be called in case a bowler is adjudged to be chucking the ball.  
13. Substitute fielders  
    1. The umpires shall allow a substitute fielder if they are satisfied that a fielder has been injured or become ill during the course of the match for any reason acceptable to them.  
    2. The participant who is being substituted on the field will be allowed to bat only after the number of overs for which he sat out while their team was fielding.  
    3. In all other circumstances, a substitute is not allowed.  
14. Batsmen are expected to step out to bat with required protective equipment (i.e. helmets, gloves, pads, abdominal guard). Umpires may pause play until batsmen are equipped with the aforementioned equipment.  
15. In case of any dispute, the latest ICC Playing Handbook will be referred to unless directly referred to in the Agneepath rulebook. [https://images.icc-cricket.com/image/upload/prd/xxc5orswsnf0sz3uo9xu.pdf](https://images.icc-cricket.com/image/upload/prd/xxc5orswsnf0sz3uo9xu.pdf)  
16. Miscellaneous  
    1. In case of disciplinary infractions, PoCs have the authority to disqualify the teams in question.  
    2. Participants are expected to bring their own equipment.   
    3. Teams are expected to report for registration on time. If they fail to do so, they may face a penalty in the form of a deduction of overs.  
    4. All rules are subject to change based on circumstances as per organisers’ discretion.  
    5. Organisers reserve the right to disqualify teams if the teams are found exhibiting unsportsmanlike behaviour or providing fraud participant data.
